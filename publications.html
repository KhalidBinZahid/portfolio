Development of an IoT Based Transformer-Centric General-Purpose Thermal and Electrical Safety System

QPAIN • 2026

Now-a-days, fire accident due to electrical short circuit is very common. This often happens because of failure of any electrical component due to excessive heat, current or voltage. To avoid these accidents, we developed a system which can monitor and control of a transformer's condition and provide realtime data using Internet of Things (IoT). Transformer's lifetime and dependability are significantly reduced by their extreme sensitive to abnormal voltage situations, overcurrent, and heat stress. This paper presents the design and implementation of a general-purpose IoT-based transformer protection system capable of monitoring temperature, current, and voltage in real time and can take preventive measures to avoid any accident. The proposed design uses an ESP32 microcontroller interfaced with an INA219 current-voltage sensor and a Dallas DS18B20 digital temperature sensor to continuously acquire and process important operational data. To ensure steady relay operation and avoid frequent switching under variable conditions, a memorybased decision method is presented. The system automatically activates cooling mechanisms and isolates the transformer load when predefined safety thresholds are exceeded. By the time, all operational data are transmitted to a cloud platform for remote visualization and supervision. Experimental results demonstrate accurate sensing and effective protective response under varying operating conditions. Here, the DS18B20's average accuracy is about 99 % and INA219's average accuracy is about 97 % with more than 96 % of accurate protective response. Although the prototype is validated on a distribution transformer, the developed architecture is scalable and can be deployed for thermal and electrical protection of a wide range of industrial and household electrical equipments.

Self-Powered Plant Monitoring System with Dual-Axis Solar Tracking, Sensor Fusion, Rain Detection, and Cloud Control for Sustainable Agriculture

QPAIN • 2026

Inefficient irrigation practices account for substantial freshwater waste in agriculture, with small scale deployments cumulatively contributing to global water and energy challenges. Here, Internet of Things (IoT) solutions offer automation potential but a fundamental research gap exists in optimizing energy autonomy and water efficiency simultaneously in low cost integrated systems. This paper proposes an adaptive scheduling framework for a self powered plant monitoring system that addresses this gap. Our methodology integrates a lightweight sensor fusion algorithm with confidence based weighting; and an energy water optimization model. The system employs dual axis solar tracking with energy aware adaptive control, multi sensor data fusion (soil moisture, temperature, humidity, rainfall, light intensity), pump dry run protection, and cloud based monitoring. Experimental validation over four weeks shows a 35.2%(±2.1%) reduction in water usage compared to timer based irrigation and a 28.4%(±3.2%) improvement in daily energy yield over fixed panel systems. Implemented with an ESP32 microcontroller at a total cost of only US50,the system achieves full operational autonomy while maintaining plant moisture within optimal ranges for 92%(±2%) of monitoring time. This work establishes a cost effective, resource optimal model for sustainable IoT agriculture. This also demonstrates that integrated feature design can overcome traditional trade offs between performance, autonomy, and cost in micro irrigation systems.

Edge-Integrated Multi-Modal Intelligent Autonomous Vehicle with Voice Commands and Obstacle Avoidance Using Deep Learning

ICECTE • 2026

Most autonomous systems operate in isolated modes without dynamic transition capabilities. This paper presents an integrated framework that combines the manual, voice, and autonomous control schemes onto Raspberry Pi and overcome the problem of fulfilling resource-limited hardware with flexible multimodal operation. The system uses SSD MobileNet V3 to provide real-time object detection with a person detection rate of 92.5% and a car detection rate of 93.5%. Moreover, a multilingual voice interface achieves recognition rates of 88.3% and 79.3% accuracy in English and Bengali, respectively. Mode switching with latencies as low as 380-1,150 ms is achieved with a responsive web dashboard. Experimental evidence shows strong performance in terms of industrial surveillance, search-and-rescue, and educational applications, whereas the overall cost of the implementation is around 211 USD (25,150 BDT). The suggested system is successful in connecting specialized robotic platforms with viable, available, multimodal solutions, and as such, provides a scalable and low-cost framework of intelligent autonomous navigation in various settings.

Real-Time Multi-Modal Embedded Vision Framework for Object Detection Facial Emotion Recognition and Biometric Identification on Low-Power Edge Platforms

ICCIT • 2025

Intelligent surveillance systems often handle perceptual tasks such as object detection, facial recognition, and emotion analysis independently, but they lack a unified, adaptive runtime scheduler that dynamically allocates computational resources based on contextual triggers. This limits their holistic understanding and efficiency on low-power edge devices. To address this, we present a real-time multi-modal vision framework that integrates object detection, owner-specific face recognition, and emotion detection into a unified pipeline deployed on a Raspberry Pi 5 edge platform. The core of our system is an adaptive scheduling mechanism that reduces computational load by 65 % compared to continuous processing by selectively activating modules such as, YOLOv8n for object detection, a custom FaceNet-based embedding system for facial recognition, and DeepFace's CNN for emotion classification. Experimental results demonstrate the system's efficacy, with the object detection module achieving an Average Precision (AP) of 0.861, facial recognition attaining 88% accuracy, and emotion detection showing strong discriminatory power (AUC up to 0.97 for specific emotions), while operating at 5.6 frames per second. Our work demonstrates that contextaware scheduling is the key to unlocking complex multi-modal AI on cost-effective edge hardware, making intelligent perception more accessible and privacy-preserving.

Multi-Class Viral Skin Lesion Diagnosis Using ConvNeXt Deep Learning with Explainable AI

BECITHCON • 2025

The global burden of viral skin diseases including Monkeypox, Chickenpox, Cowpox, Measles, and Hand, Foot and Mouth Disease (HFMD) requires accurate diagnostic solutions. But the visual similarities among lesions causes frequent misclassification in clinical settings. Traditional methods like Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) face limitations in cost, time, and accessibility. And existing deep learning approaches suffer from dataset imbalance and lack of interpretability. This paper introduces a comprehensive deep learning framework leveraging ConvNeXt Base architecture for multi-class viral skin lesion classification. The system integrates rigorous dataset validation using MD5 hashing for duplicate removal, advanced augmentation including RandAugment, and dual imbalance mitigation through Weighted Cross-Entropy Loss and WeightedRandomSampler. This is also evaluated on the Multi-Class Viral Skin Lesion Dataset (MCVSLD) containing 11,325 clinical images across six categories. Our approach achieves 99.82% accuracy and 99.85% macro F1-score, with perfect ROC-AUC and Precision-Recall AUC scores of 1.000. Gradient weighted Class Activation Mapping (Grad-CAM) provides visual explainability, confirming clinically relevant feature focus. This work demonstrates a robust, interpretable solution for automated skin disease diagnosis.

Design and Development of a Low-Cost Scalable GSM-IoT Smart Pet Feeder with a Remote Mobile Application

SPICSCON • 2025

Pet ownership is increasingly common in modern households, yet maintaining a consistent feeding schedule remains challenging for the owners particularly those who live in cities and have busy lifestyles. This paper presents the design, development, and validation of a low-cost, scalable GSM-IoT smart pet feeder that enables remote monitoring and control through cellular communication. The device combines with an Arduino microcontroller, a SIM800L GSM module for communication, an ultrasonic sensor for real-time food-level assessment, and a servo mechanism for accurate portion dispensing. A dedicated mobile application was developed using MIT App Inventor which allows owners to send feeding commands and receive real-time status updates. Experimental results demonstrate a 98% SMS command success rate, consistent portion dispensing with ±2.67% variance, and reliable autonomous operation. Its modular, energy-efficient design makes it easy to use in a wide range of households, including those with limited resources. This work pushes forward the field of accessible pet care technology by providing a practical, scalable, and completely internet-independent solution for personalized pet feeding. In doing so, it sets a new benchmark for low-cost, GSM-powered automation in smart pet products.

Design and Development of a Multifunctional Agricultural Robot Prototype for Precision Farming.

ICMIME • 2024

This document represents the design and development of a multifunctional agricultural mobile robot aimed at automating several farming tasks, including soil moisture measurement, precise irrigation, seed planting, and grass cutting. The robot uses an Arduino Nano as its central controller, with capabilities to measure soil humidity across a range of 0-100%, controlled by moisture sensors. The robot’s speed of operation is 0.5 m/s, and it can cover an area of approximately 100 m² per cycle. The robot is equipped with servo motors for accurate seed planting and grass cutting, and a simple smartphone application enables real-time monitoring and control of robot functions. The moisture sensors trigger the watering mechanism, while the robot precisely plants seeds at predefined intervals and utilizes a rotating fan blade for grass cutting, improving efficiency and reducing manual labor. The robot's seeding mechanism can plant seeds at intervals of 5 cm, enhancing planting precision. A mobile application developed via MIT App Inventor facilitates user interaction, enabling the activation of various functions like watering, seeding, and mowing, while providing live sensor feedback. This research highlights the potential of such systems in addressing labor shortages, resource optimization, and enhancing food security through precision agriculture.
